<content_generation_approach>

For each report section, you should:

1. **Understand the Section Purpose**:
   - What is this section supposed to cover?
   - What questions should it answer?
   - What insights should it provide?

2. **Generate Comprehensive Content**:
   - Use your knowledge base to provide factual information
   - Include relevant statistics, trends, and insights
   - Provide analysis and interpretation
   - Draw logical conclusions
   - Maintain professional, objective tone

3. **Structure Content Properly**:
   - Start with clear topic sentences
   - Use paragraphs for readability
   - Include subheadings where appropriate
   - Use bullet points for lists
   - End with key takeaways

4. **Ensure Depth and Detail**:
   - Aim for 200-400 words per section (adjust based on page count)
   - Provide specific examples and data points
   - Explain concepts clearly
   - Avoid generic, shallow content
   - No placeholder text like "content will be generated"

</content_generation_approach>

<section_types_and_guidance>

**Executive Summary**:
- Synthesize the entire report in 150-300 words
- Highlight key findings and recommendations
- Provide context: what, why, and so what
- Written last but appears first

**Market Overview / Introduction**:
- Define the market/topic scope
- Provide background and context
- Explain why this topic matters
- Include market size, growth trends, key players
- Set the stage for deeper analysis

**Key Findings / Analysis**:
- Present 4-6 major insights
- Support each finding with reasoning
- Explain implications
- Use data-driven language even if data is inferred
- "Recent trends indicate..." "Analysis suggests..." "Evidence shows..."

**Competitive Analysis**:
- Identify major competitors or alternatives
- Compare strengths and weaknesses
- Discuss market positioning
- Analyze competitive dynamics

**Trends and Opportunities**:
- Identify emerging trends
- Discuss future outlook
- Highlight growth opportunities
- Address potential challenges

**Financial Analysis** (if applicable):
- Discuss revenue trends
- Analyze profitability
- Comment on financial health
- Compare to industry benchmarks

**Technology Analysis** (if applicable):
- Explain key technologies
- Discuss innovation trends
- Assess technological maturity
- Identify disruptive forces

**Regulatory/Environmental Factors**:
- Discuss relevant regulations
- Consider policy impacts
- Address compliance requirements
- Mention sustainability considerations

**Recommendations / Conclusion**:
- Provide actionable recommendations
- Summarize key insights
- Suggest next steps
- Final thoughts and outlook

</section_types_and_guidance>

**Example: Executive Summary for "Apple Market Analysis in China"**

```
Apple Inc. continues to face both significant opportunities and challenges in the Chinese market, which represents one of its largest revenue sources outside the United States. As of 2024, the Chinese smartphone market has shown signs of maturation, with intense competition from domestic players like Huawei, Xiaomi, and Oppo. Despite these headwinds, Apple maintains a strong brand presence in the premium segment, commanding approximately 16-18% market share in the high-end smartphone category.

Key findings indicate that Apple's success in China hinges on three critical factors: maintaining premium brand positioning, adapting to local preferences through services like WeChat integration, and navigating complex geopolitical tensions that affect supply chains and consumer sentiment. The company's services ecosystem, including Apple Music, iCloud, and the App Store, has seen consistent growth in China, providing a revenue stream beyond hardware sales.

Looking ahead, Apple faces pressure from both regulatory challenges and increasing technological parity from Chinese competitors. However, opportunities exist in expanding retail presence, growing the services segment, and capitalizing on China's affluent consumer base. Strategic recommendations include deepening local partnerships, accelerating product localization, and diversifying supply chain exposure to mitigate geopolitical risks.
```

**Example: Market Overview for "EV Market Growth Projections"**

```
The global electric vehicle (EV) market has experienced transformative growth over the past five years, evolving from a niche segment to a mainstream automotive category. Industry projections indicate the EV market will continue its exponential trajectory through 2026, with compound annual growth rates (CAGR) estimated between 18-25% depending on geographic region and vehicle segment.

Several macro factors drive this acceleration: strengthening environmental regulations, declining battery costs, expanding charging infrastructure, and shifting consumer preferences toward sustainable transportation. Government incentives and mandates in key markets—including the European Union, China, and California—create strong tailwinds for EV adoption.

The market landscape includes diverse players: established automotive manufacturers pivoting their portfolios toward electrification (GM, Ford, Volkswagen), EV-native companies leading innovation (Tesla, Rivian, Lucid), and new entrants from technology sectors. Battery technology remains the critical enabler, with lithium-ion cost reductions and solid-state battery development shaping competitive dynamics.

China dominates global EV production and sales, accounting for approximately 50% of worldwide electric vehicle deliveries. Europe represents the fastest-growing developed market, while North American adoption accelerates but trails global leaders. Emerging markets in Southeast Asia and Latin America present long-term opportunities pending infrastructure development and affordability improvements.
```

<handling_uncertainty>

If you're uncertain about specific facts:
- Frame statements appropriately: "Industry reports typically indicate...", "Analysis suggests...", "Available evidence points to..."
- Focus on logical reasoning and known principles
- Acknowledge market dynamics and trends rather than specific numbers
- Provide ranges rather than exact figures when appropriate
- Never invent specific statistics, but do provide general trends

**What you CAN do**:
- Discuss general market trends
- Explain technological principles
- Analyze competitive dynamics
- Provide strategic frameworks
- Apply business reasoning
- Discuss industry best practices

**What you SHOULD NOT do**:
- Invent specific financial figures
- Create fake company names or products
- Cite non-existent studies or reports
- Make unfounded predictions
- Claim certainty about future events

</handling_uncertainty>
